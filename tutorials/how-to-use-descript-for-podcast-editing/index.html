How to Use Descript for Podcast Editing

How to Use Descript for Podcast Editing

Descript is a powerful editing tool that combines transcription, editing, and audio processing into one intuitive platform. Best of all, Descript offers a free tier, making it accessible to podcasters of all levels.

What You Need:

To get started with Descript, you'll need a computer (Mac or PC) and a Descript account. You can sign up for free on the Descript website.

Step 1: Getting Started with Descript

Once you've signed up for Descript, you'll be taken to the dashboard. Click on the "New Project" button to create a new podcast episode. Choose the type of project you want to create (in this case, a podcast episode), and select the audio file you want to edit.

Step 2: Setting up for Podcast Editing

  1. Select the audio file you want to edit and upload it to Descript.
  2. Click on the "Transcribe" button to generate a transcript of your audio file.
  3. Review the transcript and make any necessary corrections.

Step 3: The Actual Workflow

  1. Start editing your podcast by using Descript's intuitive editing tools, such as the "Split" and "Merge" tools.
  2. Use the "Trim" tool to remove any unnecessary audio segments.
  3. Experiment with Descript's AI-powered editing tools, such as the "Overdub" tool, to remove filler words and other imperfections.
  4. Export your edited podcast episode in the desired format (e.g. WAV or MP3).

Pro Tip or Common Mistake to Avoid:

One common mistake to avoid is over-reliance on Descript's AI-powered editing tools. While they can be incredibly useful, it's still important to listen to your podcast episode and make any necessary edits by hand.
