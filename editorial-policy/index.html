Editorial Policy

How AI Guerrilla reports, verifies, and publishes

Our Commitment

AI Guerrilla is committed to publishing accurate, original, and source-verified AI industry news. Every article published on this site has been reviewed and approved by James before going live.

Primary Sources

We publish original analysis based on primary source announcements. Our sources include:

  • Official company blogs (OpenAI, Google DeepMind, Anthropic, Meta AI, NVIDIA, Microsoft)
  • Official press releases and product announcements
  • SEC filings and regulatory documents
  • GitHub releases and open-source project announcements
  • Verified reporting from established tech press (TechCrunch, The Verge, VentureBeat, Wired, MIT Technology Review)

We do not publish rumors, unverified claims, or anonymous sources as fact.

Original Analysis

Every article on AI Guerrilla includes original analysis written from the perspective of AI operators and entrepreneurs. We do not reprint press releases. We read the source, verify the facts, and produce an original take on what it means and what to do about it.

AI-Assisted Content Policy

AI Guerrilla uses AI tools in its editorial workflow to assist with research and drafting. No AI-generated content is published without human editorial review. James reviews and approves all final content. The analysis, opinions, and editorial judgment are always human-authored.

Independence

Editorial coverage is not for sale. Businesses and products covered on AI Guerrilla do not receive preferential treatment in exchange for payment or advertising. Sponsored content, when published, is clearly labeled as such.

Corrections Policy

We correct errors promptly and transparently. If an article contains a factual error, we update the article and note the correction at the bottom of the piece.

To submit a correction, contact us at: hulljessej@gmail.com

Please include the article URL, the specific error, and any supporting documentation.
